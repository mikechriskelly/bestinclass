English Lessons

Students will be presented with activities that promote applied knowledge rather than memorization; therefore new skills taught are consistently paired with exercises apply them.

Students are encouraged to develop reading, writing, and critical thinking skills at a gradually increased level of difficulty throughout the program, allowing them to build up their vocabulary and master standard English grammar.

Once a week, students will participate in a small-class setting with college educated instructors for 60 to 75 minutes. Best in Class’ proprietary English enrichment curricula matches each student’s abilities with classmates in a fun, stimulating learning environment.

Areas of Focus

  • Reading Comprehension
  • Essay and Creative Writing
  • Vocabulary
  • Grammar & Spelling
  • Sentence and Paragraph Structure
  • Standardized Test Preparation

Math Lessons

Students are encouraged to master mathematical concepts and then apply them to real-world situations through a combination of hands-on classroom learning, supplemental homework and weekly assessment tests. This allows the instructor to proactively track progress and identify areas of improvement.

Once a week, students participate in a small-class setting for 60 to 75 minutes with a college educated math instructor. Best in Class’ proprietary math enrichment curricula matches a student’s abilities with classmates at the same level so they can improve individually, but among friends.

No matter the level, a student develops the confidence to think critically about problem solving and then apply this learning to their everyday life – ultimately preparing them for success ahead.

Areas of Focus

  • Critical Thinking and Problem Solving
  • Word Problems and Real World Applications
  • Arithmetic
  • Pre-Algebra, Algebra I and II
  • Geometry and Trigonometry
  • Pre-Calculus
