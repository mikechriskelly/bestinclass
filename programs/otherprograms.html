At Best in Class, believe creativity should be encouraged in all students and studies have revealed schoolchildren exposed to the arts often are more proficient in the core academic areas including reading, writing and math.

Select Best in Class locations offer Painting and Paper Art Classes intended to improve patterns of cognitive function and teach students methods of calm and constructive expression of ideas. Classes also offer students the opportunity to engage in fun learning and discover their own artistic talent.

Best in Class is a proud partner of Bricks4Kidz, and offers a hands-on one hour class at select locations where students build machines, buildings, vehicles and other structures out of LEGO® bricks, using one-of-a-kind model plans designed by Bricks4Kidz.

Instructional content is dynamic on a class-by-class basis and designed to increase students’ general STEM (science, technology, engineering and math) knowledge through concepts that are related to the model of the day.

Students have the opportunity to socialize with friends while learning fundamentals of engineering and architecture and developing an appreciation for how things work. All material is tailored to students’ age, experience and skill level.

A program specifically designed for students applying to the University of Washington’s Foster School of Business, to gain a competitive advantage by mastering critical thinking and effective communication in preparation for the Writing Skills Assessment portion of the application.

The course is designed to hone the skills necessary to achieve a high score on the WSA, which requires an applicant to write two strong essays in 90 minutes – a persuasive essay and a positions essay. Students will be armed with time-management strategies so they are prepared to complete their essays in the allotted time, and also will be challenged with exercises that focus on the following areas to improve overall writing ability:

  • Essay structure and organization
  • Use of appropriate tone, style, grammar and language
  • Identifying evidence to support arguments to take a meaningful position as well as persuade effectively

Available at all Washington locations, the course is separated into eight 90-minute lessons and can be taken in a private lesson format one-on-one, two students per instructor, or in a group lesson format with 3 to 5 students.
